Soya Chaap

Tender soya chaap, marinated to perfection — Deliciousness in Every Bite!

Soya Chaap is a popular vegetarian protein-rich dish made with marinated soya chaap pieces, which are cooked until tender and flavorful. The dish is a perfect blend of spices and herbs, offering a delicious and satisfying option for those seeking a vegetarian alternative to meat. It’s often served with naan, roti, or steamed rice, and is a favorite choice at Indian restaurants for its rich taste and texture.

Ingredients:

  • Soya chaap (frozen or fresh, cut into pieces)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Yogurt
  • Lemon juice
  • Coriander powder
  • Cumin powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Garam masala
  • Turmeric powder
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt
  • Sugar (optional)
  • Cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, garam masala, turmeric powder, kasuri methi, salt, and sugar (if using) to create the marinade.
  2. Coat the soya chaap pieces thoroughly in the marinade and let them sit for 30 minutes to an hour.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and cook the marinated soya chaap pieces until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  4. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  5. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
